Mae Carol Jemison

(1956 - Present)

As a doctor, engineer, and NASA astronaut, Mae Jemison has always reached for the stars. In 1992, Jemison became the first African American woman to travel in space. She has also written several books and appeared on many television programs including an episode of Star Trek: The Next Generation. In addition to her many awards, Jemison has been inducted into the National Women's Hall of Fame and the International Space Hall of Fame.

Childhood

  • Born in Decatur, Alabama
  • She moved to Chicago at the age of 3
  • Her father was a maintenace worker and her mother was an elementary school teacher

Education

  • She attended Stanford University on a full scholarship at the age of 16
  • She studies Chemical Engineering and African American Studies in Standford
  • She proceeded to get her doctorate from Cornell University

Career

  • She started The Jemison Group, a consulting company that encourages science, technology, and social change
  • She trained with Nasa and she received her first mission on September 28, 1989
  • On September 12, 1992 Jemison and six other astronauts went into space on the space shuttle Endeavor.
